Module 4: Rizal's Life: Travel Abroad and Homecoming

Lesson 1: First Travel Abroad (1882-1885)

Purpose and Context

Left for Spain to pursue higher education and broaden his horizons. This journey marked the beginning of his transformation into a reformist and writer.

Major Achievements

  • Spain (Madrid)
    • Studied medicine at Universidad Central de Madrid
    • Earned his Licentiate in Medicine
    • Completed degree in Philosophy and Letters
    • Published "Amor Patrio" in Diariong Tagalog
  • France (Paris)
    • Studied under renowned artists
    • Observed European culture and society
    • Began formulating ideas for reform
  • Germany (Heidelberg)
    • Studied ophthalmology under Dr. Otto Becker
    • Learned German language and culture
    • Started writing "Noli Me Tangere"

Lesson 2: First Homecoming and Second Travel

First Homecoming (1887)

  • Key Events
    • Returned after completing education
    • Practiced medicine in Calamba
    • Treated his mother's failing eyesight
    • Published "Noli Me Tangere" in Berlin
    • Faced growing opposition from Spanish authorities

Second Travel (1888-1891)

  • Countries Visited
    • Japan (Tokyo, Yokohama)
    • United States (San Francisco)
    • United Kingdom (London)
    • France (Paris)
    • Belgium (Brussels, Ghent)
    • Spain (Madrid)
  • Major Works
    • Published "El Filibusterismo"
    • Annotated Morga's "Sucesos"
    • Wrote articles for La Solidaridad

Lesson 3: Final Years

Second Homecoming and Exile

  • Return to Philippines (1892)
    • Established La Liga Filipina
    • Faced increasing surveillance
    • Arrested and exiled to Dapitan
  • Life in Dapitan (1892-1896)
    • Established a school for boys
    • Created water system for the town
    • Practiced medicine
    • Met Josephine Bracken

Final Journey

  • Last Days
    • Volunteered as doctor for Cuba
    • Arrested and returned to Manila
    • Imprisoned in Fort Santiago
    • Executed at Bagumbayan on December 30, 1896
