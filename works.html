Major Novels

Noli Me Tángere (Touch Me Not)

1887

His first novel exposed the corruption and abuse of the Spanish colonial government and clergy in the Philippines. The title, meaning "Touch Me Not" in Latin, refers to the social cancer of the society. Contains 333 pages of which 25 pages are nationalistic in nature and 120 pages discuss the state of the country.

El Filibusterismo (The Reign of Greed)

1891

The sequel to Noli Me Tángere, darker in tone and theme, focusing on revenge and revolution. Published in Ghent, Belgium. A more radical and revolutionary book compared to the Noli.

Essays and Articles

The Indolence of the Filipinos

1890

Published in La Solidaridad from July 15 to September 15, 1890. A scholarly essay defending Filipinos from the Spanish accusation of being lazy and indolent. Rizal explained how the tropical climate, Spanish colonial policies, and historical circumstances contributed to the alleged indolence.

Philippines A Century Hence

1889-1890

A series of essays predicting the future of the Philippines in the next hundred years. Rizal analyzed the past and present to predict four possible futures for the Philippines: remaining a Spanish colony, becoming a colony of other nations, Spain granting reforms, or the Philippines achieving independence.

Letter to the Young Women of Malolos

1889

Written to support the young women who sought to establish a night school to learn Spanish. This letter emphasizes the importance of education, mothers' roles in children's upbringing, and women's rights.

Historical Works

Annotations to Morga's Sucesos

1890

His historical annotations to Antonio de Morga's Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas, providing a Filipino perspective on Philippine history. This work demonstrated the advanced state of Philippine civilization before Spanish colonization.

Notable Poems

Mi Último Adiós (My Last Farewell)

1896

Written on the eve of his execution, this poem expresses his great love for his country and his last farewell to his family and countrymen.

A La Juventud Filipina (To The Filipino Youth)

1879

Written when he was 18, this poem calls on Filipino youth to use their talents for the betterment of the country.

Sa Aking Mga Kabata (To My Fellow Children)

1869

One of his earliest poems, written at age eight, emphasizing the importance of one's native language.

Letters and Correspondence

Letters to Family

1876-1896

Extensive correspondence with his family, especially his parents and sisters, during his travels and exile. These letters provide intimate insights into his thoughts, experiences, and family relationships.

Letters to Blumentritt

1886-1896

Correspondence with his close friend Ferdinand Blumentritt, discussing politics, science, and culture. These letters reveal Rizal's intellectual depth and his views on various subjects.

Scientific Works

Studies on Philippine Flora

1890-1896

Scientific studies and drawings of Philippine plants and animals, particularly during his exile in Dapitan. These works demonstrate his contributions to natural science.

Medical Notes and Observations

1887-1896

Various medical writings and observations from his practice as a physician, including his time in Dapitan where he treated many patients.
