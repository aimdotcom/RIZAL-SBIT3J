Module 5: Rizal's Life: Exile, Trial, and Death

Lesson 1: Exile in Dapitan (1892-1896)

Life in Exile

After his deportation to Dapitan, Rizal made the most of his exile through various achievements and contributions:

Major Accomplishments

  • Educational Initiatives
    • Established a school for boys
    • Taught modern methods of education
    • Emphasized both academic and practical skills
    • Influenced many young minds
  • Community Development
    • Created a water supply system for Dapitan
    • Improved town planning and sanitation
    • Initiated agricultural projects
    • Enhanced local infrastructure
  • Medical Practice
    • Treated numerous patients
    • Performed successful eye surgeries
    • Provided free medical care to the poor
    • Gained reputation as a skilled physician
  • Scientific Research
    • Conducted studies on local flora and fauna
    • Collected specimens for European museums
    • Corresponded with international scientists
    • Made significant scientific discoveries

Lesson 2: The Trial

Legal Proceedings

  • Charges Filed
    • Rebellion
    • Sedition
    • Conspiracy against the government
  • Court Martial Process
    • Trial dates: December 26-29, 1896
    • Defense by Lt. Luis Taviel de Andrade
    • Lack of direct evidence linking to revolution
  • Rizal's Defense
    • Maintained innocence
    • Emphasized belief in peaceful reform
    • Rejected violent revolution

Lesson 3: Final Days and Death

Last Hours

  • Cell in Fort Santiago
    • Wrote his final poem "Mi Último Adiós"
    • Married Josephine Bracken
    • Reconciled with Catholic faith
  • Final Morning
    • Date: December 30, 1896
    • Time: 7:03 AM
    • Location: Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park)
  • Execution
    • Requested to face firing squad
    • Last words: "consummatum est" (it is finished)
    • Shot in the back as Spanish authorities considered him a traitor
