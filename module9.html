Module 9: Dr. Jose Rizal and Philippine Nationalism

Lesson 1: Bayani and Kabayanihan

Understanding "Bayani"

According to Dr. Zeus Salazar (1997), the local concept of bayani has a different value and is richer than its western counterpart "hero". The "héroe" could be likened to a martyr who may or may not think of compatriots when making actions. A "héroe" commonly acts as an individual and is viewed to be exceptional.

Concept of "Kabayanihan"

  • Community Spirit
    • Acts with collective concern in mind
    • Focuses on common good
    • Emphasizes unity and cooperation
  • Active Heroism
    • Helping without expecting returns
    • Volunteering for community
    • Supporting collective achievements
  • Filipino Values
    • Pakikisama (Solidarity)
    • Pagdamay (Shared responsibility)
    • Pakikipagkapwa (Unity with others)

Lesson 2: Rizal and Philippine Nationalism

Rizal's Nationalism

  • Personal Experience
    • Witnessed injustices firsthand
    • Family faced persecution
    • Experienced colonial oppression
  • Response to Oppression
    • Used education and skills for reform
    • Wrote to expose colonial abuses
    • Promoted peaceful resistance
  • Legacy of Reform
    • Inspired Filipino consciousness
    • United Filipinos for freedom
    • Promoted education and progress

Lesson 3: National Symbols and Recognition

National Heroes Commission Criteria

The former president Fidel V. Ramos enacted E.O Number 75 on March 28, 1993, establishing the National Heroes Commission (NHC). The criteria for choosing a national hero include:

  • Heroes must:
    • Have a concept of nation and aspire for freedom
    • Define and contribute to a system of freedom and order
    • Contribute to the quality of life and destiny of a nation
    • Think of the future generations

National Symbols

  • Official Recognition
    • Rizal Day (December 30)
    • Rizal monuments nationwide
    • Republic Act 1425 (Rizal Law)
    • Presence in Philippine currency
