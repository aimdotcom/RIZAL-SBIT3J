Module 1: Introduction to the Course: Republic Act 1425

Lesson 1: Republic Act 1425

Background and Purpose

The Rizal law, also known as "Republic Act 1425", mandates all higher local and state educational institutions (HEIs) in the Philippines to include the Rizal course in their curriculum. This course covers The Life, Works and Writings of Dr. Jose Rizal, with particular emphasis on his two famous novels "Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo".

Key Figures in the Law's Creation

  • Senator Claro M. Recto
    • Authored "Bill 438" (Rizal Bill) - his first authored bill
    • Advocated for mandatory inclusion of Rizal's works in education
    • Led the push for comprehensive study of Rizal's life and works
  • Senator Jose B. Laurel Sr.
    • Chairman of the Committee on Education
    • Received and interpreted the bill
    • Facilitated congressional readings and debates

Lesson 2: Issues and Controversies

Catholic Church Opposition

  • CBCP's Concerns
    • Violation of Canon Law 1399
    • Conflict with Catholic doctrine
    • Concerns about anti-clerical content
  • Specific Objections
    • Attack on church hierarchy
    • Challenge to core Catholic faith
    • Controversial religious content

Resolution and Compromise

The Rizal bill faced significant opposition but was eventually modified through careful revisions:

  • Key Compromises
    • Novels not mandatory in basic education
    • Compulsory only at college level
    • Focus on patriotic and peaceful philosophy

Lesson 3: National Hero Selection

Historical Context

Interestingly, no specific law explicitly proclaims Dr. Jose Rizal as the National Hero of the Philippines. His status emerged from his significant contributions to Philippine social transformation and the widespread recognition of his impact.

Selection Process

  • Taft Commission Criteria
    • Established during American regime
    • Set standards for national hero selection
    • Considered multiple candidates
  • Notable Candidates
    • Dr. Jose Rizal
    • Antonio Luna
    • Marcelo H. Del Pilar
    • Graciano Lopez Jaena
    • Emilio Jacinto
